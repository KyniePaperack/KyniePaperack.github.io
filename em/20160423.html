 Activate MyLifeOrganized Pro.

Okay, so, MyLifeOrganized is the desktop thing I use to make to-do lists, organize projects, place in dreams and whatnot. I use a modified Get Things Done™ system. Yes, that is trademarked. It's a system technically owned by a guy who wrote a book on it named David Allen. Now, David Allen is a businessman, so he works with a lot of restrictions in location and work because a lot of business transactions and meetings, you can't do just...anywhere, so most of the concepts in his book, Getting Things Done, is written with the context being "most things can't be done everywhere". Now, the thing is, in our modern world, where we have phones in our pockets with a ridiculous amount of power (such as having access to the world's largest collection of human knowledge at a whim), this book is extremely outdated. It's almost absurd how impractical it is now, since a hell lot of work can be done and finished with a phone, especially on large-screen phones such as mine.

This dates the book, but the concepts are fairly simple to grasp. I've gotten the concepts without trudging through the, what most people would consider to be, single most painfully boring, useful book ever written (this is due to the supposedly absurd amount of business language and jargon used in the book). That's because the system, in general is quite easy to grasp. C.G.P. Grey sums it up quite nicely in Hello Internet, during Episode 39, but I'll put it down here, for ease of access:

  • Capture everything.
  • Process what can be done with it.
  • Organize it all and sort into what can be done and not.
  • Reflect on what needs to be done.
  • Do it.

It's surprising how well it works, but the problem part is the @context system, where things are given "contexts" (formatted: @<location/person/object>). This can potentially be a very useful thing, but it is driven by accessibility to work, which, as previously stated, can be very easy to access almost anywhere, rendering the entire thing practically useless.

My modifications to the system include the removal and/or reuse of the context system for other things, such as what that particular item on the list is for. I have also made an unintentional modification, mainly due to inaccessibility and lack of funds for it. I have been, fairly recently, saving up a lot of money for various purposes (some of which, you'll find in the to-do list itself).
